Actions

The Mute / Solo buttons on each filter row in the toolbar popup, and what they affect.

M Mute — hides posts of that type (AI slop, promoted, newsletters, hiring, reaction reshares, job / anniversary / certification announcements). The post collapses to a small stub you can expand. Turn Mute on for any filters you want gone.
S Solo — the “show me just this” mode: shows only that type and hides everything else, so you can audit what a filter is catching. While any Solo is on it overrides the Mutes. Example: Solo “Hiring posts” → your feed shows only hiring posts.

Post controls

The icon buttons on each hidden‑post stub. AI slop, Hide post, and Mute retire the row from your feed; Always show reveals the post (and removes the stub).

AI slop — confirm this post really is AI slop: it trains the filter (a stronger signal than “Show anyway”) and hides the row. The button shows a checkmark as it retires the post.
Hide post — retire just this one row from the feed, without muting the author or training the filter. A quick way to clear a stub you’ve dealt with.
Mute — a soft block: you won’t see this author’s posts again — they’re filtered out of your feed completely (works for people, companies, and schools). Manage or undo under Muted authors below.
Always show — always show this author; never filter them again. Their posts appear normally, with no FeedHacker stub or controls. They’re added to the Whitelist below, where you can remove them.
Visit profile — open the author’s LinkedIn profile in a new tab (where you can unfollow/block/report in LinkedIn’s own UI). FeedHacker never automates that.

How AI-slop detection works

FeedHacker doesn't hide a post for a single word. It scores each post across the structural “tells” below plus a curated phrase list, weighs the evidence with an on‑device model, and only hides once the combined score reaches the threshold (0.5). The weights shown are your current learned values — they nudge each time you hit “Show anyway” or re‑hide a post.

Flagged phrases

The curated banlist of words and phrases that read as AI‑written. Confirmed phrases count strongly. Aggressive ones match common words, so they always apply but count as weaker evidence — the sensitivity slider governs how much they filter. Manual entries are guidance only and are not auto‑matched.

    AI-slop decision log

    FeedHacker tunes itself: it reviews the posts you see and, on its own, down‑weights “tells” that fire on most of your feed (so one common signal can’t flag everything) and sets the threshold so only the sloppiest slice is hidden — no clicking required (toggle in Advanced). This is a local record of every post it flagged and why — the probability, the tells that fired, and the phrases matched — plus your verdict if you hit Show anyway / the ✓ splat. Everything stays on your computer; export it to send for a deeper, global tune.
